Digital crash course

To give you a good grounding in all things agile and digital, we recommend the below as a good start. If you already know your stuff, you might want to look at our more specific recommendations.

Introductory reading

  • Nesta - Radical Visions of Future Government, Sept 2019
  • The Agile Manifesto, 2001
  • Definition of Digital, Tom Loosemore, 2016
  • Agile concepts series, Martin Aspelli (Deloitte Digital)
  • Human centred design kit
  • Strategic thinking with blogging and stickers, Giles Turnbull, 2018

Interactive learning

  • Learn to code - GDS's free, interactive and practical introduction to building websites with HTML and Ruby
  • The government's online Skills Toolkit is a great resource for some practical learning on digital skills

Digital in government

  • Government Digital Service Manual
  • Don't bring policy and delivery closer together, make them the same thing, James Reeve, 2017
  • Making government as a platform real, Tom Loosemore, 2018
  • Reframing our politics for the digital age, Mark Thompson, 2019

  • Government as a platform (video)

Working with digital teams

  • Goals, cycles and people: running an agile, complex programme in government, Janet Hughes, 2016
  • Digital is something you are, Giles Turnbull, 2017

Technology

  • What is code?, Paul Ford, 2015
  • Government technology innovation strategy, Government Digital Service, 2019
  • Getting tech savvy (requires organisation log in)

Relevant LinkedIn Learning

Remember to login to LinkedIn learning with your organisation account, not your personal one!
